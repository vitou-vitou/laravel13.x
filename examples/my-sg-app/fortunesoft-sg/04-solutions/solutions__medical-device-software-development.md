 Yes, Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) must comply with regulatory approvals from authorities like the FDA, MDR (EU), and other governing bodies. 4. Are systems like EHR considered SaMD? 

 No, EHR systems are not classified as SaMD. SaMD functions independently of hardware, whereas EHR systems primarily store and manage patient data. 5. How much does it cost to develop software for medical devices? 

 The cost varies based on complexity, compliance, and features. Custom, high-compliance solutions require higher investment. 6. Do we need a real SaMD software development device? 

 Not necessarily. SaMD can be developed and tested using simulations, but real-device validation is required for regulatory approval. 7. What are the types of medical device software?

 • Remote Monitoring & Wearable Software
